What Is A Certified Business Intermediary & How It Helps You Sell

A Certified Business Intermediary (CBI) is a professional designation awarded by the International Business Brokers Association (IBBA) to brokers who meet strict education, experience, and ethical standards.

Simply put, it’s the gold standard in the business brokerage industry. Working with a CBI means you’re partnering with someone who understands the ins and outs of valuations, negotiations, and closing deals the right way.

Frequently Asked Questions

What Paperwork Is Needed To Sell A Business In Sun City?

Common paperwork includes financial statements, tax returns, lease agreements, employee records, and a list of assets. Buyers also expect detailed operational information, contracts, and any legal documents tied to the business. With a broker, you can organize these to keep the process smooth and confidential.

How Long Does It Take To Sell A Small Business?

On average, selling a small business can take six to twelve months. The timeline depends on factors like industry, asking price, market demand, and how prepared the seller is with documentation. Working with an experienced broker often helps speed up the process.

What Factors Determine A Business’s Value?

Value depends on profitability, financial history, customer base, industry trends, and growth potential. Tangible assets like equipment and inventory, as well as intangible factors like reputation and contracts, also influence pricing. A professional valuation ensures your Sun City business is accurately positioned in the market.

What Are The Steps To List A Company For Sale?

The process includes preparing financial records, conducting a business valuation, creating marketing materials, and listing confidentially to attract buyers. From there, negotiations begin, due diligence is completed, and final closing paperwork is handled.

What Information Do Buyers Want Before An Offer?

Buyers usually want recent financial statements, tax records, details about employees, lease agreements, customer contracts, and an overview of operations. They also look for information about growth opportunities and risks. The clearer and more organized this information is, the more attractive the business becomes.

Will I Owe Taxes After Selling My Business In Arizona?

Yes, taxes are typically owed after selling, depending on your business structure, profit, and sale terms. Capital gains taxes may apply. Consulting with an accountant or tax advisor is essential to understand your specific obligations and minimize liability when selling in Arizona.

How Much Time And Effort Is Involved In The Selling Process?

Selling requires consistent effort in gathering documents, meeting with potential buyers, and managing negotiations. With a broker’s guidance, much of the workload is handled professionally, reducing stress for the seller. On average, expect several months of preparation, communication, and review before reaching the closing table.
